# 1. Product Summary

**Potential Names:**

- Build Guild
- Earn A Slice

**Domains**

- buildguild.ai
- earnaslice.com
- helpbuildthis.com
- helpme.build

**Taglines**

- Ship work. Earn royalties.
- Build together, share the upside.

**One-liner**

> Earn A Slice lets founders ‚Äúopen-source‚Äù parts of their startup: they post real work (growth, marketing, product, etc.), contributors help ship it, and everyone who contributes earns an ongoing **share of the upside**, not just a one-off payment.

**What it is**

- A platform where:
  - **Founders** create **Projects** (their products/companies).
  - They define a **Reward Pool** (e.g. 10% of Oath Notes profits).
  - They publish **Bounties** (specific tasks with point rewards: growth, sales, content, design).
  - **Contributors** claim bounties, deliver results, and earn **Points** tied to **measured performance**.
  - Points convert into **recurring payouts** out of that pool.

**What it‚Äôs not (on purpose)**

- Not a generic freelance marketplace.
- Not a ‚Äúfind a cofounder‚Äù app.
- Not a token/crypto play.
- Not complex cap table equity; this is **off-cap-table, rev/profit-share**.

**Initial target users**

Prior Assembly Made members: https://github.com/orgs/asm-products/people

# 2. Problem & Opportunity

### Founder Problem

- Solo or tiny founding teams can build impressive infra/product‚Ä¶
- ‚Ä¶but get stuck on **distribution, growth, marketing, and sales**.
- Hiring full-time is expensive and risky.
- Traditional agencies/freelancers are misaligned:
  - Paid hourly or per deliverable.
  - Incentive to bill, not to generate **durable revenue**.

- Equity feels too permanent and binary:
  - Either ‚Äúno equity‚Äù or ‚Äúhere‚Äôs 10‚Äì20% in perpetuity.‚Äù

**Result:** It‚Äôs easier to ship product than to build a repeatable growth engine.

### Contributor Problem

- Skilled people (growth, content, sales, design, dev) want:
  - **Leverage**: upside in things they help grow.
  - A way to build a **portfolio** of product contributions.
  - A model between ‚Äúfull-time job‚Äù and ‚Äúcold freelance gig.‚Äù

- They lack:
  - A structured way to **earn ongoing upside** in multiple products.
  - Clear rules for **how contributions turn into $$** (vs vague ‚Äúwe‚Äôll figure out equity later‚Äù).

### Opportunity

- Create an **Assembly-style** platform for 2025+:
  - Real products.
  - Clear contribution rules.
  - Programmatic rev/profit sharing tied to **performance**, not vibes.

- Think: **GitHub for startups**, where contributors earn royalties instead of GitHub stars.

Solo founders or small teams with a built or shipping product who:

# 4. Core Product Concept & Mental Models

Think about it as three combined metaphors:

1. **GitHub repo** ‚Äì A project you can "open up" to others.
2. **Bounty board** ‚Äì Specific, well-defined outcomes that are up for grabs.
3. **Royalty / rev-share engine** ‚Äì People who contribute to results share in ongoing upside.

### GitHub Mental Model

Most contributors already understand GitHub. We use familiar patterns:

| GitHub       | Earn A Slice     | Notes                                         |
| ------------ | ---------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Repository   | **Project**      | The product/company being built               |
| README.md    | **Readme**       | About, mission, links (Discord, assets, docs) |
| Issues       | **Bounties**     | Specific tasks with point rewards             |
| Contributors | **Contributors** | Auto-computed from accepted submissions       |
| Pull Request | **Submission**   | Contributor's work + proof                    |
| Merge        | **Approval**     | Founder approves ‚Üí points awarded             |
| Sponsors     | **Reward Pool**  | The $ that flows to contributors              |

**Key Concepts (for users, not engineers):**

- **Project** ‚Äì The thing you're building (e.g. "Oath Notes").
- **Readme** ‚Äì Markdown-style about page: mission, how to help, links to Discord/resources.
- **Bounties** ‚Äì Concrete ways to help with clear rules and point rewards (e.g. "Bring in a new clinic that stays 90+ days: 100 points").
- **Contributors** ‚Äì Anyone who has earned points via approved submissions (auto-computed, like GitHub's contributor list).
- **Reward Pool** ‚Äì The slice of revenue/profit reserved for contributors (e.g. "10% of net profit").
- **Points** ‚Äì Your share of the Reward Pool. The more points you collect, the bigger your cut.
- **Payouts** ‚Äì Regular distributions of the Reward Pool to all contributors, in proportion to their points.

# 5. Value Proposition (per user type)

### For Founders (like you with Oath)

- **Turn your product into a magnet for talent**
  Attract people who are good at growth, content, sales, etc. by giving them upside, not random promises.

- **De-risk growth experiments**
  You only pay when things work (e.g., a new clinic stays 90 days, or MRR actually increases).

- **Keep control & cap exposure**
  You choose:
  - What % of revenue/profit is shared.
  - What counts as a qualifying contribution.
  - When and how payouts are done.

- **Narrative benefit**
  ‚ÄúWe run Oath like an open-source company. Anyone who helps us grow shares in the upside.‚Äù

---

### For Contributors

- **Get paid for outcomes, not time**
  If you‚Äôre good at bringing revenue or users, your work can compound, not just pay once.

- **Stack small bets**
  Work with several projects; some pay a little, some might break out and pay for years.

- **Portfolio & reputation**
  Show:
  - ‚ÄúI helped Oath grow MRR by $X.‚Äù
  - ‚ÄúI have Y points and Z total lifetime earnings across projects.‚Äù

1. **Founder wants growth help without hiring FTE**

   - Founder sees who's actually generating results (Contributors tab).

   - Invite page explains:
     - What Oath does.

   - Contributors submit:
     - ‚ÄúI closed Clinic ABC; here‚Äôs proof.‚Äù
     - ‚ÄúHere‚Äôs a case study we shipped that drove this clinic.‚Äù

   - You decide:

6. **Run a payout**
   - At quarter end, you:
     - Enter: net profit or revenue for Oath Notes.
     - Platform shows how the 10% pool is split by points.

   - You approve and pay via your existing payment channels (MVP: manual).

7. **Iterate**
   - Add more Bounties as you see what works:
     - "Launch webinar series."
     - "Land a partnership that drives 3+ clinics."

   - Adjust point values and rules as you learn.

# 8. MVP Product Surfaces

Laser-focused on the **bounty ‚Üî reward loop**. Three core surfaces per project, plus a simple Discover page.

---

### 8.1 Project Page

Each project has one page with three tabs:

#### Tab 1: Readme (default view)

Like a GitHub README. Founder writes this in markdown-ish format.

- **Mission / About**: What the product is, why it exists
- **How to Help**: What kinds of contributions are valuable
- **Links**: Discord, docs, brand assets, website
- **Reward Pool Summary**: "10% of net profit ‚Üí distributed monthly"

#### Tab 2: Bounties

Like GitHub Issues. The core of the reward loop.

- **List of open bounties**, each showing:
  - Title: "Close a new clinic (90-day retention)"
  - Points: "+100 points"
  - Tags: `growth`, `sales`, `content`, `design`
  - Status: Open / Claimed / Completed
  - Description: what's required, how success is verified

- **Filters**: by tag, by status
- **Sort**: by points (highest first), by date

- **Claim flow**: Contributor clicks "I'm working on this"
- **Submit flow**: Contributor submits proof ‚Üí Founder approves/rejects ‚Üí Points awarded

#### Tab 3: Contributors

Auto-computed from approved submissions. Like GitHub's contributor graph.

- **List of everyone who holds points**, showing:
  - Avatar + username
  - Points held
  - % of total pool
  - Lifetime earnings from this project

- Sorted by points (descending)
- **This IS the team**‚Äîno separate setup required

**Privacy:**

- Submissions are **private by default**‚Äîonly the contributor and founder can see them
- Other contributors cannot see each other's submissions or evidence
- This protects sensitive information (sales contacts, strategy details, proprietary work)

**Submission contents:**

- **Text description**: What was done, context, notes
- **Attachments**: Screenshots, documents, files (stored in R2)
- **Links**: External evidence (Stripe dashboard, CRM records, published content)

## 9. Pool Expansion, Dilution & Fairness

This section explains how contributor ‚Äúdilution‚Äù works, why it‚Äôs acceptable (and often positive), and how founders should structure goals/milestones to keep everyone focused on real revenue and outcomes.

### 9.1 Mental Model

- Each Project has a **Reward Pool** (e.g. ‚Äú10% of net profit from Oath Notes‚Äù).

- Contributors earn **Points** for delivering verified outcomes.

- Each payout period, the Reward Pool is split **pro-rata by points**:

  > `User Share = Pool Amount √ó (User Points / Total Points)`

- When new points are granted to new contributors, **existing contributors‚Äô percentage share decreases** (dilution) but **their absolute payout can still increase** if the pie (revenue/profit) grows enough.

**Key principle:**

> **Dilution is acceptable when expanding the contributor pool creates more total value than it subtracts in percentage ownership.**
> If new contributors grow revenue/profit, existing contributors should earn _more money_, even if their share of the pool shrinks.

### 9.2 What ‚ÄúExpanding the Pool‚Äù Means

In Earn A Slice v1:

- The **Reward Pool %** (e.g. 10% of profit) is set by the founder and only changed explicitly.
- ‚ÄúExpanding the pool‚Äù normally means:
  - Adding **new contributors** and
  - Granting **new points** when they produce verified, revenue-linked outcomes.

This **does not** retroactively change past payouts; it only affects **future periods**.

### 9.3 When Dilution Is Fair vs. Unfair

**Fair dilution (intended case):**

- New contributors:
  - Bring in more clinics, customers, revenue, or durable growth.
  - Hit well-defined, pre-agreed milestones.

- Result:
  - Total profit/revenue increases.
  - Reward Pool in dollar terms grows.
  - Existing contributors‚Äô percentage share shrinks, but their **payouts stay flat or rise**.

**Unfair dilution (what the system should avoid):**

- Points are granted:
  - For vague or unmeasured work.
  - For contributions that don‚Äôt drive durable value.
  - Without clear rules communicated up front.

- Result:
  - New people dilute the pool **without** growing the pie.

**Product stance:**
Earn A Slice's UX and defaults should **strongly encourage / nudge** founders toward fair dilution by:

- Making **performance-based Bounties** the default.
- Requiring clear **verification criteria** for each Bounty.
- Emphasizing that **no verified outcome = no points = no dilution**.

### 9.4 Guidance for Founders: How to Expand the Pool

When founders want to bring in more people (e.g. more growth partners, designers, etc.), the product should guide them to:

1. **Announce expansion clearly**
   - Example copy in UI:

     > "You're adding new Bounties and allowing new contributors to earn points. Existing contributors' percentage share may decrease, but the goal is for the overall pie (revenue/profit) to grow enough that everyone benefits."

2. **Tie new points strictly to performance**
   - Every new Bounty should:
     - Be linked to a measurable outcome (MRR, active clinics, activated accounts, etc.).
     - Have a **verification window** (e.g. 90-day retention).
     - Award points only on confirmation of that outcome.

3. **Avoid ‚Äúfluffy‚Äù point grants**
   - Avoid awarding points for:
     - Ideas without implementation.
     - One-off artifacts (a slide deck, an email draft) without evidence of impact.

   - Where necessary, label such work as **fixed-fee** instead of point-based.

4. **Use examples to calibrate**
   - In the UI, provide templates such as:
     - ‚ÄúNew clinic active 90 days ‚Üí 100 points.‚Äù
     - ‚ÄúCase study that leads to a clinic within 120 days ‚Üí 50 points.‚Äù
     - ‚ÄúHit $10k net-new MRR this quarter ‚Üí 500 point bonus.‚Äù

### 9.5 Contributor-Facing Fairness Messaging

Earn A Slice should include a concise, reused block of copy explaining fairness and dilution to contributors. For example:

> - You earn **points** when you deliver verified outcomes (like new customers, revenue, or key milestones).
> - Each payout period, a fixed % of the product‚Äôs revenue/profit is shared among contributors proportional to their points.
> - As new contributors join and earn points, your **percentage** of the pool may decrease, but our goal is that the **total pool grows** because of everyone‚Äôs work.
> - If new contributors don‚Äôt generate results, they don‚Äôt earn points‚Äîand can‚Äôt dilute you.
> - Rules for earning points are public and defined before you do the work.

This should be visible on:

- Project onboarding pages.
- The ‚ÄúHow this pool works‚Äù modal.
- Contributor terms/FAQ.

### 9.6 Example Scenario (for UX copy / education)

**Before expansion:**

- Pool: 10% of net profit.
- Profit this quarter: $40,000 ‚Üí Pool = $4,000.
- Total points: 4,000.
- Early contributor holds 1,000 points (25%) ‚Üí receives **$1,000**.

**After expansion:**

- New contributors join and are awarded points only after landing net-new clinics and growth.
- Profit next quarter: $120,000 ‚Üí Pool = $12,000.
- Total points: 6,000.
- Early contributor still has 1,000 points (now ~16.7% of pool) ‚Üí receives **$2,000**.

**Message to users:**

> ‚ÄúYour percentage of the pool went down, but your payout doubled because the product grew. Pool expansion is designed to be positive-sum when new contributions create real revenue.‚Äù

### 9.7 Guardrails & Defaults

To keep everyone reward/revenue-focused, Earn A Slice should:

- Default new Bounties to **outcome-based templates** (e.g., revenue, active users, retained customers).
- Encourage **verification windows** (90‚Äì120 days for revenue-linked work).
- Make **manual point grants** (not tied to any Bounty) an explicit advanced action with warnings:
  - ‚ÄúThis may dilute existing contributors without clear performance criteria.‚Äù

- Provide a short ‚ÄúBest Practices for Founders‚Äù guide when they first create a Reward Pool, emphasizing:
  - Pay for results, not hours.
  - Don‚Äôt grant points retroactively without explaining to current contributors.
  - Consider separate pools for distinct products/initiatives to avoid cross-dilution.

## 12.6 Project Lifecycle

What happens when projects change or end.

### Scenario: Startup Pivots

- If the product pivots significantly, the founder can:
  - Continue the existing pool (if the pivot is minor)
  - Close the current pool and start a new one for the new direction
- Existing points remain valid for any remaining payouts before closure
- Founder should communicate changes to contributors

### Scenario: Startup Dies

- If the startup shuts down, points become worthless (no profit = no payouts)
- This is expected and should be clear to contributors from the start
- No payout obligations remain after shutdown

### Scenario: Startup Is Sold / Acquired

- Reward pools **do not automatically transfer** to the acquiring company
- Founder should:
  - Run a final payout before or at closing
  - Clearly communicate that the pool is ending
  - Optionally: negotiate with acquirer to honor existing contributor relationships

### Pool Commitment & Duration

Pools have a **defined commitment period** set at creation. This protects contributors from surprise terminations.

**At pool creation, founder chooses:**

- Commitment length: 6 months, 1 year, 2 years, or 3 years (default: 1 year)
- This is the minimum period the pool will run
- Displayed prominently to contributors: "This pool runs through December 2027"

**During the commitment period:**

- Founder cannot unilaterally terminate the pool
- Pool continues as long as the product exists and has profit to distribute
- Early termination only allowed for qualifying events (see below)

**Qualifying early termination events:**

- Startup shuts down (no business = no pool)
- Startup is sold/acquired (see acquisition section above)
- Mutual agreement with contributors (rare, requires transparency)

**Extending the pool:**

- Founders can extend the pool at any time‚Äîno need to wait until the end
- Extension adds another commitment period (6mo/1yr/2yr/3yr) from current date
- Existing contributors and their points carry forward seamlessly
- Great for founders who are happy with their team and want to keep the momentum

**At commitment end (if not extended):**

- Founder can extend for another term, or
- Let the pool expire naturally with 90-day notice to contributors
- Final payout is run for any outstanding profit
- Points become inactive after final payout

**Contributor visibility:**

- Pool end date is displayed prominently on every bounty
- Before claiming, contributors see: _"This pool runs through June 2025 (3 months remaining)"_
- Contributors make an informed choice‚Äîif 3 months of payouts isn't worth the work, they can skip it
- If founder extends, everyone keeps earning

**Why this matters:**

Contributors need to trust that the upside they're working toward won't vanish arbitrarily. A defined commitment period makes the deal clear upfront and holds founders accountable.

### Modifying Pool Terms

- Founders can change pool terms (%, payout frequency) with notice
- Changes only affect **future periods**, never retroactive
- Contributors should be notified of any changes
